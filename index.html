Snap-Draw Holsters

Scroll down for more info

Our product was inspired by the recent wave of backlash on our police departments in America, as well as a recent news story about a deputy pulling a gun while off duty. We wanted to help reduce tensions and make it easier to provide evidence in court. In the case where body cameras or security cameras are not functioning properly or present, we need a tamper-proof way to tell what exactly happened in the heat of the situation. That's why we came up with the Snap-Draw holsters: a cheap and easy method to keeping track of a firefight. This easy and ready to produce device that can send Bluetooth signals about when a gun has been removed or put back in its holster. Once fully developed, it could be used for tracking the number of shots fired, and when the safety is turned off. Keep in mind that the device is designed for police security, we support our deputies and wish them luck in their careers. This is for everyone's safety, including theirs. For more information, scroll down.

The way our product works is whenever you pull out a gun from its holster, it will track the amount of times you have done so. The mechanism behind it is a small button located within the holster. Everytime the gun is taken out of a holster, or put back in, the button will be pressed the action will be tracked. The button will then send a signal using bluetooth. The information will be sent to the police department. What we aim to do, later on, is to create a way to track the amount of times the safety has been turned off or the amount of times a bullet has been fired.
